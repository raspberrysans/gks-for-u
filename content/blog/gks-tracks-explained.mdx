GKS-U has six sub-tracks. Most online guides lump them together or only describe the most popular three. This post is the complete reference: every sub-track, with quotas, eligibility nuances, eligible universities, and the type of applicant each is designed for.

If you haven't decided between the Embassy and University umbrella tracks yet, start with the [Embassy Track vs University Track guide](/blog/embassy-track-vs-university-track).

## The six tracks at a glance

| Sub-track | Umbrella | Quota (2025) | Universities | Field |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| General | Embassy | 71 | Type A (29) + Type B (39), choose 3 | All |
| Overseas Koreans | Embassy | 7 | Same as General | All |
| IRTS | Embassy | 9 | Same as General | All |
| R-GKS | University | 80 | 39 regional | All |
| UIC | University | 100 | 8 institutions | Science/Engineering only |
| Associate Degree | University | 100 | 9 institutions | Vocational |
| **Total** | | **367** | | |

## Embassy Track sub-tracks

### 1. General

**Quota (2025):** 71 scholars distributed across 68 countries.

**Who it's for:** Most applicants. If you're not Ukrainian and not an ethnic Korean abroad, this is your default Embassy Track option.

**Universities:** Choose **up to 3** from the combined Type A + Type B list. **At least 1 must be Type B** (regional). You cannot apply to three Type A universities.

- **Type A (29 institutions):** Ajou, Chung-Ang, Dankook, Dongguk, Duksung Women's, Ewha Womans, Hankuk University of Foreign Studies, Hansung, Hanyang, Hongik, Incheon National, Inha, Konkuk, Kookmin, Korea, Kyung Hee, Myongji, Seokyeong, Seoul National, SeoulTech, Shinhan, Sogang, Sookmyung Women's, Sungkyunkwan, Sungshin Women's, Tech University of Korea, UNIST, University of Seoul, Yonsei.
- **Type B (39 institutions):** Andong National, Busan University of Foreign Studies, Cheongju, Chonnam National, Chosun, Chungbuk National, Chungnam National, Daegu Catholic, Daegu, Daejeon, Dong-A, Dongseo, Gyeongsang National, Hannam, Hoseo, Inje, Jeju National, Jeonbuk National, Kangwon National, Keimyung, Kongju National, Konyang, Korea Maritime & Ocean, Korea University Sejong Campus, KOREATECH, Kunsan National, Kyungpook National, Kyungsung, Mokwon, Pai Chai, POSTECH, Pukyong National, Pusan National, Semyung, Sila, Sun Moon, Sunchon National, University of Ulsan, Yeungnam.

**Per-country allocation (2025 cycle):** Most invited countries received exactly **1 General seat**. The exceptions, with **2 General seats**, were: Vietnam, Mongolia, Russia, Brazil, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Mexico, Uzbekistan, and a few others. Angola received 2.

**Profile fit:** Strong all-rounders, prestige seekers, and applicants from countries with multiple seats. The realistic competition is your country's strongest applicants.

### 2. Overseas Koreans

**Quota (2025):** 7 scholars from 7 countries.

**Who it's for:** Ethnic Koreans living abroad who hold non-Korean citizenship. Adoptees, descendants of Korean emigrants, and others with documented Korean lineage.

**Universities:** Same combined Type A + Type B list as General. Same "choose 3, at least 1 Type B" rule.

**Required documents:** Family register, birth certificate, or other government-issued document proving your relationship to a Korean lineal ascendant. NIIED also explicitly favors Overseas Koreans applicants in evaluation preferences.

**Profile fit:** If you have Korean heritage and proper documentation, this is **almost always a better path than General**, because the pool is far smaller (7 seats globally, but spread across only 7 countries with 1 seat each).

### 3. IRTS — International Reconstruction Talent Scholarship

**Quota (2025):** 9 scholars, **Ukrainian nationals only**.

**Who it's for:** Ukrainian applicants. The IRTS program was created under the Korea-Ukraine MOU to support Ukraine's reconstruction by educating Ukrainian talent in Korea.

**Universities:** Same combined Type A + Type B list as General.

**Procedural note:** The Ukrainian government conducts the first round of selection. All applicant documents are submitted via the Korean embassy and must meet NIIED submission requirements.

**Profile fit:** If you're Ukrainian, IRTS gives you 9 dedicated seats with no competition from non-Ukrainian applicants. Ukraine also gets 1 Overseas Korean seat, but IRTS is the primary path.

<Callout variant="info" title="picking among embassy sub-tracks">
You can apply to **only one** of General, Overseas Koreans, or IRTS — not multiple. Most applicants don't have a real choice (you either qualify for OK / IRTS or you don't). If you do qualify for OK or IRTS, those tracks have dramatically smaller pools and better odds than General.
</Callout>

## University Track sub-tracks

### 4. R-GKS — Regional GKS

**Quota (2025):** 80 scholars, **6 regions**.

**Who it's for:** Applicants from invited regional countries who are open to studying at regional universities outside the Seoul metropolitan area. R-GKS is a deliberate policy lever: NIIED uses it to channel international students to regional Korean cities that would otherwise lose enrollment.

**Universities (39):** The Type B list above.

**Regional quotas (2025 cycle):**

| Region | Countries (count) | Quota |
|---|---|---|
| Southeast Asia | 11 (Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Timor-Leste, Vietnam) | 22 |
| Northeast Asia | 1 (Mongolia) | 7 |
| Southwest Asia | 7 (Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka) | 27 |
| Central Asia | 5 (Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan) | 11 |
| The Americas, Europe, the Pacific | 28 | 5 |
| The Middle East, Africa | 22 | 8 |

**Profile fit:** The best track for most Southeast/Southwest/Central Asian applicants who are open to a regional university. Southwest Asia in particular has favorable per-region odds (27 seats across 7 countries). Mongolia gets 7 seats just for itself.

**Application:** One university, one department. Once you commit, you can't switch later.

### 5. UIC — University Industry Cooperation

**Quota (2025):** 100 scholars worldwide. **No country or region quotas — open to all invited countries.**

**Who it's for:** Science and engineering applicants. UIC programs are explicitly built around industry collaboration: curriculum is designed with industry partners, and students participate in field experience and lab work alongside their coursework.

**Universities and majors (8 institutions):**

| University | Department(s) | Degree |
|---|---|---|
| Ajou University | Software Engineering | Bachelor's |
| Konyang University | Smart Security | Bachelor's |
| Kookmin University | College of Computer Science (Software, Artificial Intelligence) | Bachelor's |
| KOREATECH | Mechanical Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering | Bachelor's |
| Pusan National University | Mechanical Engineering, Industrial Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering, Nanoenergy Engineering, Nanomechatronics Engineering, Optics and Mechatronics Engineering, Biological Sciences | Bachelor's |
| Tech University of Korea | Mechatronics Engineering | Bachelor's |
| Kyungbok University | Big Data | Associate |
| Yeungjin University | Division of Computer Information, AI Convergence Mechanical Engineering | Associate |

**Profile fit:** STEM applicants targeting AI, computer science, mechanical engineering, mechatronics, software, or related fields. UIC's 100-seat global pool is genuinely competitive but not country-bottlenecked — your competition is the best applicants in the world for these specific programs, not just from your country.

**Bonus point:** Science/engineering applicants automatically get **5% bonus points** on the evaluation rubric, on top of UIC's STEM-friendly screening.

<Callout variant="info" title="uic vs r-gks for stem">
Some universities (like Pusan National, KOREATECH) appear in both UIC and R-GKS for similar departments. UIC has narrower department options but is open to all countries. R-GKS has broader university options but is region-bottlenecked. If your country is in the Americas/Europe/Pacific (5 R-GKS seats for 28 countries), UIC is usually the better play.
</Callout>

### 6. Associate Degree

**Quota (2025):** 100 scholars. Open to **74 invited countries**, with **no country or regional quotas**.

**Who it's for:** Applicants pursuing a two-year vocational degree in a technical field. The Associate Degree track is structurally distinct: it's for shorter, applied programs at vocational institutions rather than research universities.

**Universities (9):** Dong-Eui Institute of Technology, Gumi University, Hanyang Women's University, Hosan University, Korea University of Media Arts, Kyungbok University, Osan University, Vision College of Jeonju, Yeungjin University.

**Program length:** 1 year of Korean language + 2–3 years of associate degree program (vs 4–6 years for bachelor's degrees).

**Bonus point:** **Winners of national-level competitions on technical skills** receive evaluation preference — a unique boost for Associate Degree applicants.

**Profile fit:** Applicants who want a faster, more applied path into the Korean workforce. Associate-degree graduates are also eligible to later apply to bachelor's-level GKS-U as a different cycle's applicant. Skill competition winners (e.g., WorldSkills, national vocational champions) have a particular edge here.

## Cross-track comparison: realistic odds

If you have a flexible profile and could plausibly apply through more than one track, this rough table compares "raw" odds. Take it with appropriate skepticism — actual odds depend on the strength of each year's applicant pool, not just quotas.

| Sub-track | Seats | Approx. global pool | Raw % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Embassy General (2 seats / country) | 2 | 50–100 | 2–4% |
| Embassy General (1 seat / country) | 1 | 30–80 | 1–3% |
| Overseas Koreans | 7 | ~50 | 14% |
| IRTS (Ukrainian only) | 9 | ~100 | 9% |
| R-GKS Southeast Asia | 22 | ~600 | ~4% |
| R-GKS Southwest Asia | 27 | ~700 | ~4% |
| R-GKS Mongolia | 7 | ~150 | ~5% |
| R-GKS Americas/Europe/Pacific | 5 | ~400 | ~1% |
| UIC | 100 | ~3,000–4,000 | 2–3% |
| Associate Degree | 100 | ~1,000–1,500 | 7–10% |

The Associate Degree pool tends to be smaller because most applicants default to bachelor's tracks — which is exactly the inefficiency that makes Associate Degree statistically attractive.

<Callout variant="warn" title="don't pick a track on raw odds alone">
Track odds are only one input. The other inputs are: (1) whether the universities you actually want are reachable through that track, (2) whether you'll write a credible study plan in that field, and (3) whether you can sustain four years of study in that program. A 10% chance at a track you'll fail to commit to is worse than a 3% chance at one that fits your goals.
</Callout>

## How to make the final pick

Walk through these decisions in order:

1. **Are you Ukrainian?** Apply IRTS (Embassy).
2. **Do you have documented Korean lineage and non-Korean citizenship?** Apply Overseas Koreans (Embassy).
3. **Do you want a top-tier metro university (SNU, Yonsei, Korea, Sungkyunkwan, etc.)?** Apply Embassy General — it's the only path.
4. **STEM applicant whose target major is at one of the 8 UIC universities?** Apply UIC.
5. **Open to a regional university and your country/region has favorable R-GKS quotas?** Apply R-GKS.
6. **Want a 2–3 year vocational program?** Apply Associate Degree.
7. **None of the above clearly fits?** Default to Embassy General.

## What you submit is the same across tracks

Regardless of which sub-track you apply through, the document set is identical: **Forms 1–6** plus required certificates. The differences are *where* you submit (embassy vs university), *how many universities* you list, and *what extra evidence* you need (Overseas Korean docs, etc.).
